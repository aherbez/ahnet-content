Sagea SIGGRAPH demo


This was a prototype content aggregation system which we demoed at SIGGRAPH 2016 as part of the VR Village, and was created along with Blair Erickson, Stephanie Riggs, and Bill Booth. I was responsible for the C++ implementation of the system within the Unreal engine.

How it works

The system supported a number of different types of content, including:

  • static images
  • 2d video
  • 3d video
  • 360 degree video
  • 3d 360 degree video

Each piece of content is loaded onto its own piece of geometry and positioned relative to one of several walls. The system by which that happens borrows a page from bootstrap in that each of the walls spans a 12x9 virtual grid. Assigning a given piece of content to a given wall requires specifying a pair of coordinates for the top-left as well as a number of grid units for the width and the height.

So, the call to send a given piece of content to fill the front wall looks like: 

content->SetPosition(0, 0, 12, 9);
...making it really easy to throw content onto various walls of the space at various locations and sizes.

There are also two special case options for screens:

  • a large, partially rounded, IMAX-like display
  • a full sphere suitable for 360 degree videos
Both of those options are displayed at a single location, and only one of them can be displayed at a time.

Once we had all of that working, we implemented a number of different use cases, all tied to what a Reddit or Facebook (or similar) experience might look like in VR, as well as a menu system to choose between the different experiences.
