Starfield simulation

This demo draws a large number of stars shooting out from the center, similar to the Windows 3.1 stars screensaver. It also displays performance stats, both the current frames per second as well as the total number of stars being rendered at the momment vs the target number of stars. The difference between the two is intentional, as the app gradually adds stars to meet the target number, instead of just adding them all at once. Adding all the stars at once produced a sharp ring at the begining, which I thought didn't look quite as nice as the results from adding them a few hundred at a time.

Instructions: Use the arrow keys to adjust the number of stars. The application starts out at 30,000 stars, and the maximum range (while keeping the frame rate in the mid-40s) ranges between 50,000 (Chrome) and 72,000 (Firefox).

  • Up Arrow: increase the target number by 100
  • Down Arrow: reduce the target number by 100
  • Left Arrow: reduce the target number by 500
  • Right Arrow: increase the target number by 500
